A). Definition of novel genes

According to the junction usage, we can categorized 2,428(325 + 2,103) novel multi-exon isoforms into four groups. In order to find completely novel multi-exon genes, we check the novelity of their junctions. If all junctions of an predicted isoform are novel (i.e. not reported by RefSeq, Ensembl, KnonwGen or Gencode), it is defined as an isoform from a novel genes (the red group).

Four groups of novel isoforms

You can download these novel isoform groups at this webpage. All isoforms are divided as the Figure A below. The download links of different groups are highlighted in the same colors of the piechart


  • Isoforms of novel genes (273)
    • ALL junctions in each isoform are NOT annotated by RefSeq, Ensembl, KnownGen or Gencode.
  • Novel isoforms of exisiting genes with novel junctions (655)
    • AT LEAST ONE junctions in each isoform are NOT annotated by RefSeq, Ensembl, KnownGen or Gencode.
  • Isoforms that are novel combinations of existing junctions (876)
    • ALL junctions in each isoform are annotated by RefSeq, Ensembl, KnownGen or Gencode. But their combinations are NOT annotated by these libraries.
  • Fragment of existing isoforms (624)
    • The junction linkage of each isoform is part of the annotated junction linkages in RefSeq, Ensembl, KnownGen or Gencode.

B). Relatively high expression in hESC (H1)

273 novel isoforms are from 216 novel gene loci. The abundance of these 213 gene loci are compared between H1 and 16 human tissues by abundance ratio:
abundance ratio = gene abundance in human tissue

The standard deviation and the mean of 16 abundance ratios of each gene locus were computed and ploted (Figure B). 146 novel genes (68%, inside the red box) have averaged abundance (among 16 human tissues) ratio smaller than 0.5 with standard deviation smaller than 0.5. Human Pluripotent Associated Transcript (HPAT)

To confirm that the novel genes have expression specific to hESC, we further characterized 23 of those most expressed. For this purpose we examined expression by qPCR in two independent hESC lines (H1 and H9); one induced pluripotent stem cell line (iPSC line RiPSC.HUF1) and a collection of cDNAs obtained from different adult human tissues. All 23 targets, denoted as HPAT (Human Pluripotent Associated Transcript) genes, were abundantly expressed in the pluripotent cell lines (H1, H9, RiPSC.HUF1) as opposed to adult tissues where they were either undetectable or expressed at very low levels.


Y axis: relative gene expression (the ratio over the endogenous)
